SHRAPNEL Cinematic Trailer - I was not involved in the production of this trailer.

SHRAPNEL was originally planned to be a crypto-video game, and there was a lot of exploration into monetization strategies early in production, such as investigating ways to successfully market NFTs that were going to be tied to in-game assets. I supported these R&D efforts through technical asset visualization and rendering optimization work by helping find new workflows that would work around a NFT-based pipeline. This deepened my knowledge of Unreal Engine's rendering capabilities, including Movie Render Queue, Unreal's Python API, and Cvar systems for controlling render quality. I gained valuable experience in headless rendering workflows and high-quality asset presentation.

SHRAPNEL gave me valuable insight into game production, asset management, and team collaboration. I gained experience converting animated Skeletal Mesh vehicles into Nanite Static mesh-based systems for performance while preserving animations, optimizing Metahuman assets, and an introduction to using Unreal Insights for profiling. This project strengthened my weight painting, scripting, and cross-team collaboration skills while deepening my understanding of scalable asset workflows for high-quality game production.

I contributed to the early production phases of Shrapnel and am grateful for the opportunity to work with such talented people and for the experience I gained during my time there. My involvement concluded earlier than expected due to project funding changes.

Responsible for all character skinning, character integration, some vehicle integration, cloth simulations, renderings, and optimizations.
