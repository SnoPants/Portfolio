Neon Machine Inc. - Shrapnel

During my time at Neon Machine Inc., I worked as a Character Technical Artist on Shrapnel, a competitive, extraction-based first-person shooter developed in Unreal Engine 5. This near-future sci-fi title focuses on tactical resource extraction, high-stakes combat, and persistent progression.

My responsibilities included collaborating with the Character Lead and Character team to achieve artistic and technical goals for the game’s characters. I managed and refined Metahuman-based character assets, developed cloth simulation and character customization systems, and contributed to R&D efforts. I also worked with the UI/UX team on rendering high-quality NFT weapon and character skins, defining best practices for real-time asset presentation within Unreal Engine. Additionally, I partnered with the Art Director on lighting setups and collaborated with the environment team to produce Nanite-based vehicles.

Shrapnel was my first professional video game project, and it gave me valuable insight into game production—chaotic, challenging, and rewarding. I strengthened my weight painting and scripting skills, learned how to manage thousands of character and clothing assets efficiently, and grew into a stronger Unreal technical artist overall.

One key learning experience involved converting an animated Nanite vehicle into a skeletal mesh due to runtime CPU costs. This taught me a valuable lesson: Nanite assets aren’t always optimal out of the box, especially when socket complexity and asset count are high. Although I didn’t see the project through to its final release, it progressed to multiple early pre-beta stages and worldwide playtests—an invaluable experience that shaped how I approach performance and optimization challenges today.
